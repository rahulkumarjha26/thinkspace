Brand is just a perception, and will match reality over time

Rahul Jha & Smriti Tiwari Nov 11, 2020 ∙ 3 minutes read

When it comes to building a brand, it's important to understand that a brand is not just a logo or a tagline, but a perception that consumers have in their mind. It is the sum of all experiences, interactions, and associations that a customer has with a company and its products or services. And this perception is constantly evolving over time.

In the past, brands were built on traditional marketing efforts such as advertising, public relations, and sponsorships. However, as technology continues to advance and consumers become more savvy, the way brands are built and maintained is also evolving. The rise of social media and the increasing importance of customer reviews and user-generated content have given consumers a greater say in how brands are perceived.

"A brand is the set of expectations, memories, stories and relationships that, taken together, account for a consumer's decision to choose one product or service over another." - Seth Godin

Consumers are now more informed than ever before, and they have easy access to a wealth of information about products and services. This means that brands can no longer rely solely on traditional marketing methods to shape consumer perception. Instead, they must focus on building a strong, authentic brand that resonates with consumers. One key aspect of building a strong brand is ensuring that it aligns with reality. This means that the products and services a brand offers must live up to the promises that are made to consumers. If a brand's perception doesn't match reality, consumers will quickly lose trust in the brand and it will be difficult to regain that trust. In the future, technology will play an increasingly important role in shaping brand perception. Virtual and augmented reality, for example, will allow brands to create immersive experiences for consumers that go beyond the physical world. This will enable brands to better connect with consumers on an emotional level and create deeper, more meaningful relationships. Another important aspect of brand perception in the future will be the use of artificial intelligence and machine learning to personalize the customer experience. Brands will be able to use data to understand consumer preferences and tailor their interactions with them in real-time. This will enable brands to build trust and loyalty with their customers, which is crucial for long-term success.
